Japanese Maple Tree

Description

This is a woody plant is a native to Japan.They come in many varieties. It has leaves with four to five pointed lobes. The top side of the leaves range from bright greens to blazing reds,whilst the underside of the leaves take green color.

Shrub

It looks like a dome with more or similar lobular leaves.

Tree

It can grow from 6 to 50 feet high and about 15 to 20 feet wide.

Growth

Maple trees and shrubs thrive really well in the sun,but do take to part shade as well. They do love well drained soil. They make great container plants as well. The lifespan of these trees varies.

Landscape


The shrubs offer a good focal point and privacy. So its no suprise that they are often a prefered choice by landscapers and home owners.

The trees provide great shade and are hardy. Hence they serve as a great option for residential and commercial landscaping.

Maintenance

As any new plant, Japanese Maple needs lots of water and plenty of sunshine in its first year. Once their root establises, they do not really need any maintenance. In the fall, their leaves fall off. In the following spring, they sprout new ones back.
